Project Overview

A Digital Dining Experience

Jaffeiz Restaurant needed a website that would reflect the warmth and quality of their dining experience. They wanted an online presence that would make potential customers hungry just by browsing, while also providing practical features like menu viewing and table booking.

We built a fully responsive restaurant website that showcases their food photography, displays their menu in an organized way, and makes it easy for customers to find location details and make reservations directly through the site.

Client Requirements

What the Client Needed

Jaffeiz Restaurant wanted a website that could do more than just look good — it needed to serve as a practical tool for both the restaurant and its customers.

Digital Menu Display

An easy-to-navigate online menu with categories for appetizers, main courses, desserts, and beverages. Each item needed descriptions and pricing.

Table Reservation System

A simple reservation form where customers could book tables by selecting date, time, and number of guests. Confirmation to be sent via email or phone.

Location & Contact Info

Embedded Google Maps integration, clear contact details, and business hours prominently displayed for easy access.

Food Gallery

A visually appealing gallery section to showcase food photography and the restaurant ambiance, helping attract customers.

Mobile Optimization

Since most customers would browse on their phones, the site needed to be fully responsive with easy tap targets and fast loading on mobile data.

Customer Reviews

A testimonials section displaying real customer feedback and ratings to build trust with new visitors.

Our Solution

How We Solved It

We created a vibrant, mobile-first website using pure HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The design uses warm colors and rich typography to reflect the restaurant's brand identity. The menu section features category tabs for easy filtering, and the reservation form collects all necessary details with form validation.

We integrated Google Maps for location, added a photo gallery with lightbox functionality, and included social media links for Instagram and Facebook where the restaurant actively posts their daily specials.

Development Process

How We Built It

01. Discovery & Planning

We visited the restaurant to understand their brand, ambiance, and what makes them unique. We reviewed their menu structure and discussed how they wanted customers to experience their brand online.

02. UI/UX Design

We designed a warm, appetizing interface using colors and typography that complement the restaurant's physical branding. Wireframes focused on easy navigation and quick access to the menu and reservation features.

03. Frontend Development

Using semantic HTML5, modern CSS3 with custom animations, and vanilla JavaScript, we built a fast, lightweight website. The menu section features dynamic filtering and the gallery uses a lightbox plugin for a polished experience.

04. Content Integration

We populated the menu with all food items, uploaded professional food photography, added the restaurant's story and team information, and integrated social media feeds.

05. Testing & QA

We tested the reservation form thoroughly, checked all menu links, verified map integration, and tested responsiveness across various devices and browsers.

06. Deployment & Launch

We deployed the site, set up the domain, configured SSL, and trained the restaurant staff on how to update menu items and manage reservation inquiries.

Challenges Faced

Overcoming Obstacles

Challenge

Organizing a large menu with many categories in a way that's easy for customers to browse without feeling overwhelmed by choices.

Solution

We implemented a tabbed menu interface with smooth category switching. Each category is clearly labeled, and items are displayed in a clean grid with pricing clearly visible.

Challenge

Balancing high-quality food photography with fast loading times — restaurant sites need beautiful images without being slow.

Solution

We optimized all images using modern formats with lazy loading. The gallery uses thumbnail previews and loads full-resolution images only when clicked.
